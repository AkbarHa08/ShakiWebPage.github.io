Salis Mammadov

Salis Mammadov was born on July 1, 1932 in the city of Nukha (now Sheki). In 1948, he began working as an informant at the Nukha Radio Information Office, where he worked until 1951, when the Nukha Radio Broadcasting Office was abolished. From 1952 he was a literary worker in the editorial office of the Nukha Workers' newspaper, and from 1962 in the Nukha inter-district newspaper Lenin's Flag. In 1959 he entered the correspondence department of the journalism faculty of the Azerbaijan State University, where he graduated in 1965. During his student years, under his mediation, Bakhtiyar Vahabzadeh's poem "Gulustan" was published in the "Nukha Fehlesi" newspaper. In April 1965, by the decision of the bureau of the Nukha city party committee, the "Ipekchi" of the Nukha Silk Factory He was appointed editor of the newspaper and served as its editor for 44 years until January 2009. In December 1990, he renounced the traditional founders of Ipekchi and declared the newspaper the "Open Tribune of Sheki Silk Workers". removed the slogan. This was an unprecedented event in Azerbaijan until then, but only a rare event in the USSR. Salis Mammadov was a member of the editorial board of the "Correspondent" bulletin, a body of the Union of Journalists of Azerbaijan. In 1993, together with his son Aydin Mammadov, he founded the first independent regional newspaper, The Voice of Sheki, and was its editor-in-chief until his death.
