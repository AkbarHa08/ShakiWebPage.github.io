Abdulali bey Amirjanov

Abdulali Shirali bey oglu was born in 1870 in Sheki. In 1876–1883 ​​he studied at the Sheki city school. After graduating from the Teachers' Institute in Tbilissi (Tbilisi) in 1888, he worked as a teacher in Sheki, Lankaran and Baku. Together with Teymur bey Bayramalibeyov, he carried out many cultural and educational events. Amirjanov has worked as a teacher, accountant in government agencies and various companies in Baku since the beginning of the 20th century, was closely involved in the work of cultural and educational societies, was a member of the inspection commission of the Nashri-Maarif society and deputy chairman of the Baku Muslim Charity Society. He joined the political process in Russia after the February Revolution (1917). He was a member of the Interim Executive Committee of Baku Muslim Public Organizations. During the genocide against the Turks in Baku in March 1918, he rendered exceptional services in saving thousands of Azerbaijanis from death and preventing the massacre in the city. His older brother, Gasim bey Amirjanov, a graduate of Moscow University's law faculty and a well-known lawyer, was killed by Armenians. Amirjanov was the Minister of Finance, State Supervisor (October-December 1918) in the second government cabinet of the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic (June-October 1918). He was elected to the Parliament of the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic (December 1918) in accordance with the "Law on the Establishment of the Azerbaijan Majlis-Prisoner" of the National Council of Azerbaijan (November 19, 1918). Amirjanov, a member of the neutral group, was a member of the parliamentary budget and inquiry commissions. O, In the middle of 1919 he took part in the establishment of the "Deyanat" company in Baku to establish trade relations with foreign countries. After the April occupation, he emigrated to Istanbul and became a member of the Azerbaijani National Center operating there. He died in 1948 in Istanbul. Abdulali bey was married to Saltanat khanum Alibeyova. He had sons named Fuad bey, Rustam bey, Rashid bey, Murad bey, daughters named Valiya khanum and Maryam khanum.
