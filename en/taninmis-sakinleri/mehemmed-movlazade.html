Mahammad Movlazade

Mahammad Hasan was born in 1854 in Sheki. He received his first education in Sheki mollakhana. He then continued his education in Ganja madrasa. After graduating from the madrasa, Mohammad Hasan worked as a mullah in the Ganja Juma Mosque for a year. He later decided to continue his education and went to Iraq for this purpose. Here, Mohammad Hassan further enriches his religious education. He returned to the Caucasus in 1891 and published the Hijri and Gregorian calendars together in Persian. In 1893, he began teaching the rules of Islam (Sharia law) at the "Muslim Religious School" in Tbilissi (Tbilisi). Later, like Gazi, he worked in Jabrayil, Ganja, Tbilissi and Kutaisi. Haji Mammadhasan, who received the rank of Akhund, was a veteran for some time, and since 1907 he has been a substitute for Sheikh-ul-Islam. In 1908, Sheikh-ul-Islam was elected.
