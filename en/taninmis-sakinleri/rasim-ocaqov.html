Rasim Ocagov

Ojagov Rasim Mirgasim oglu (November 22, 1933, Nukha - July 11, 2006, Baku) - Azerbaijani film director, cinematographer, screenwriter, People's Artist of Azerbaijan (1982), Honored Artist of the Azerbaijan SSR (1967) and Chechen-Ingush MSSR (1964), USSR Laureate of the State Prize (1981), Laureate of the State Prize of the Azerbaijan SSR (1980). Rasim Ojagov was born on November 22, 1933 in the city of Sheki, Azerbaijan. From 1951 to 1956 he studied at the Faculty of Cinematography in Moscow. Then he graduated from the faculty of directing of the Aliyev Academy of Arts. From 1956 he worked as a production operator at the Azerbaijanfilm film studio, and from 1973 as a production director. In the late 50s, he began shooting at the Baku Film Studio. For the first time in 1957 as an operator " Actor Shahmar Alakbarov gave the image of Qatir Mammad. Although there were no objections to the release of the film, the success of the film as a whole was widely reported in the press. His second directorial work was The Sound of the Trumpet. "The Sound of the Trumpet" brought a lot of innovations to Azerbaijani cinema in terms of its original scenes, shooting style, artistic solution of the theme. After that, R. Ojagov created a real image of his contemporaries in the films "Birthday", "Closed Door", "Other Life", "Park", "Forgive me if I die", "Both pilgrimage and trade", "Investigation". , urges not to turn a blind eye to the negative developments in society. His latest films, such as "Tahmina" and "Hotel Room", also touch on serious topics. still talks about man's attitude to the world around him. Ojagov died in Baku in 2006.
