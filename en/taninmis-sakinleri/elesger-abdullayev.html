Alasgar Abdullayev

Alasgar Abdullayev or Shekili Alasgar (1866 - April 1, 1929) - Azerbaijani singer. Alasgar Abdullayev was born in 1866 in Sheki. He earned money by building as a child. While in Sheki, Kashtazli Hashim accidentally met A.Abdullayev. During his stay in Sheki, he taught him mugam and played a major role in his formation as a singer. Another teacher of A.Abdullayev was amateur singer Baku merchant Aga Said oglu Agabala. Shekili Alasgar took part in the 1st, 2nd and 3rd "Oriental Concerts" held in Baku in 1902-1903, sang "Chahargah", "Shushtar", "Bayati-Shiraz" mughams. He also took part in the breaks of theatrical performances in Baku, in the "Nights of the East" organized by the "Nijat" society. In 1912-1918, A.Abdullayev's concerts took place in different cities of Central Asia, Iran and Turkey. He was awarded a medal by the Shah for his performance in one of the Iranian assemblies. Alasgar Abdullayev was invited to Tbilissi (Tbilisi) and Kiev in 1913-1914 by Sport-Record and Extrafon joint-stock companies. Accompanied by tar players Gurban Pirimov and Tevos, and kamancha by Sasha Oganezashvili and Moses Gulians, he performed "Chahargah", "Rast", "Mahur-Hindi", "Zabul-Segah", "Rahab", "Shushtar", "Hasar" and others. . He writes mughams, a number of Azerbaijani, Georgian and Armenian songs. For many years he performed accompanied by tar player Shirin Akhundov. Alasgar was especially famous for singing "Rahab". After the singer Deli Ismayil, Alasgar was the first in the history of Azerbaijani music to sing "Rahab". A. Abdullayev and Jabbar Qaryagdioglu performed in front of Baku workers as part of the first Oriental orchestra organized by Dr. Ioanesyan. In addition, he performed with tar player Gurban Pirimov and kamancha player Armenak Shushinsky at summer clubs in Baku, "Oriental Music Nights". The singer also took part in the music programs of the Azerbaijani radio. Shekili Alasgar died on April 1, 1929 in Baku.
