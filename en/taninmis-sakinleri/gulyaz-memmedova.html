Gulyaz Mammadova

Mammadova Gulyaz Zakir gizi was born on May 1, 1970 in Boyuk Dahna village of Sheki region. In 1977-1987, he studied at Boyuk Dahna Secondary School No. 1. In 1988-1992 he studied at the College of Music under the Azerbaijan National Conservatory (former Music College named after A.Zeynalli). In 2002, he studied at the Azerbaijan State University of Culture and Arts, faculty of "solo singing" singing. From 1991 to March 1, 2014 he worked as a soloist at the Azerbaijan State Song Theater named after R. Behbudov. He has been a soloist of the National Opera and Ballet Theater since 1992. Since 1996 he has been working as a teacher at the College of Music under the Azerbaijan National Conservatory. He currently teaches at the College of Music (formerly Asaf Zeynalli) at the Azerbaijan National Conservatory and the Baku Humanitarian College. Leyli in Uzeyir Hajibeyli's opera "Leyli and Majnun", People's Artist of the Republic of Azerbaijan, Order of Glory, Professor Mansum Ibrahimov (June 23, 1993), People's Artist Baba Mahmudoglu, Zahid Guliyev, Honored Artist Sabuhi Ibayev (2001) September), Ilkin Ahmadov and others. In general, Gulyaz Mammadova has played the role of Leyli 105 times so far. This was followed by Shahsanam in Zulfugar Hajibeyli's opera "Ashig Garib", Telli in Uzeyir Hajibeyli's opera "Arshin mal alan", Gulbahar in Shafiqa Akhundova's opera "Bride's Rock", singer in Ramiz Mustafayev's opera "Vagif", He created the image of a singer in Uzeyir Hajibeyli's opera "Koroglu", a singer in Vasif Adigozalov's opera "Natavan" and a singer in Niyazi's ballet "Rast". On May 28-30, 2012, at the invitation of the Embassy of Azerbaijan in Georgia, he gave concerts in Tbilisi and Kutasi. In 2012, he toured Turkmenistan seven times at the official invitation. From June 7 to 1-6, 2012, at the invitation of NAYO-Norway-Azerbaijan youth organization, he participated in the Sagane International Festival in Oslo, Norway with a mugam ensemble led by Honored Artist of the Republic of Azerbaijan Sahib Pashazade.
