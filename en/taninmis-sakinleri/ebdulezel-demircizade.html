Abdulazal Demirchizade

Abdulazal Demirchizade Mamedoglu was born in 1909 in Sheki. He received his primary and secondary education in Sheki, first in a new type of school called "Haqiqatul-maarif", and then in "Shaki exemplary labor" school. After graduating from the Sheki Teachers' Seminary, he began his career as a teacher in the city school No. 2 in September 1925. His first article was published in the "New School" magazine in the same year. In his youth, Demirchizadeh showed great interest in art, wrote a number of poems, stories and essays. However, he was more interested in scientific work and therefore focused all his activities on scientific research. He came to Baku in 1929 and entered the pedagogical faculty of AUL. Here B. Chobanzade, A. He learned from prominent scientists and writers such as Hagverdiyev, YV Chamanzaminli, A.Shaig. After graduating from that faculty, he returned to Sheki and continued his teaching with great sympathy. The passion for science never A. He did not leave Demirchizade comfortable. He returned to Baku in 1933 and was admitted to the "Languages" department of API. In 1936, he published his first book, "Turkish language", and after graduating from graduate school, in 1938, he published his second book, "Summaries of the history of the Azerbaijani literary language." Prominent scientists of the time YE Bertels, SEMalov and I.K. Dmitriyev said that the last book deserved to be defended as a candidate's dissertation. In 1940, A. Demirchizade was awarded the degree of Candidate of Philological Sciences. Continuing his scientific research in the field of language history, the researcher defended his work "History of the Azerbaijani language (ancient period)" in 1944. In 1945, he was awarded the title of Professor. A. From 1940 to 1943, Demirchizadeh headed the "Azerbaijani Linguistics" departments at AUL, and from 1943 to the end of his life at API. In 1955 he was elected a corresponding member of the Academy of Sciences of the Azerbaijan SSR.
