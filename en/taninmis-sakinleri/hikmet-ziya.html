Hikmat Ziya

Efendiyev Hikmet Ziya oglu (May 13, 1929, Nukha - August 2, 1995, Baku) - Azerbaijani poet, translator, member of the Writers' Union of Azerbaijan (1958), member of the Union of Journalists of Azerbaijan (1959), leading educator (1978), honored art of the Azerbaijan SSR employee (1986). Hikmet Ziya was born on May 13, 1929 in the city of Sheki, Azerbaijan. After graduating from secondary school No. 1 in Aghdam, he studied at the journalism department of the philological faculty of AUL (1947-1952). He worked as a literary worker, head of the literature and art department in the editorial office of the "Azerbaijan Pioneer" newspaper (1952-1969), head of the poetry department in the editorial office of the "Dove" magazine (1969-1984), executive secretary (1984). "Pioneer of Azerbaijan" to literary work in 1952 It began with his first poem, Gogola, published in a newspaper. Then he appears in periodicals from time to time. His first satirical poems, including "My Father's Gift" (1957), "Is Spring Beautiful or Winter?" (1959), "Heart of a Fly" (1960) were published under the signature of Hikmet Efendiyev. The plays "Test", "Twin Brothers" were staged. He composed music for his librettos ("Grandma's Tale", "The Adventures of Two", "Pale Flowers") and his poems. His works have been translated into the languages ​​of the peoples of the former USSR. He himself took care of literary translation. He took part in children's and youth book weeks held in different cities of the country, as well as trips by train, and anniversary celebrations. "Mozalan" He is a member of the artistic council of the satirical film magazine, the All-Union Council of Children's and Youth Literature, the editorial board of the "Azerbaijan Pioneer" and "Literary Azerbaijan" magazines. Efendiyev Hikmet Ziya oglu died on August 2, 1995 in the city of Baku, Azerbaijan.
