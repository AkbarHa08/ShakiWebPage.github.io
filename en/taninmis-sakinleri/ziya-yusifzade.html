Ziya Yusifzade

Ziya Yusifzadeh was born on February 15, 1929 in Nukha (now Shaki) in an intellectual family. In 1945, he graduated from high school in Zagatala with a gold medal. In 1952 he graduated from the Moscow State Pedagogical Institute of Foreign Languages ​​with a degree in English. In 1952-1956 he held responsible positions in the Ministry of Education of the Azerbaijan SSR and the Council of Ministers of the Azerbaijan SSR. In 1956, he was drafted into the State Security Committee under the Council of Ministers of the Azerbaijan SSR. From 1972 to 1976, he was the head of the counter-intelligence department of the KGB, and from 1976, he was the first deputy chairman of the KGB. From June 18, 1980 to August 11, 1988 he served as chairman of the State Security Committee of the Azerbaijan SSR. In 1977, Major General, In 1984 he was promoted to the rank of lieutenant general. In 1988-1992 he worked as the head of the Department of International Relations in the Council of Ministers of the Azerbaijan SSR, and in August 1992 he retired at his own request. Taking into account his great life and operational experience, he was reinstated in the military service on June 28, 1999 by the Order of the President of the Republic of Azerbaijan Heydar Aliyev and was appointed head of the Academy of the Ministry of National Security. He was elected a deputy of the Baku Soviet, the Supreme Soviet of the Azerbaijan SSR and the Supreme Soviet of the USSR. Candidate for membership in the Central Committee (CC) of the Communist Party of Azerbaijan in 1970-1988, member of the CC, candidate for the bureau of the CC, In 1980-1988 he was a member of the bureau of the Central Committee. He knew four foreign languages. He was an honorary employee of the USSR State Security Service and the Ministry of National Security of the Republic of Azerbaijan. He died on September 29, 2015 in the city of Baku. After the farewell ceremony was held at the Cultural Center of the Ministry of National Security on September 30, 2015, the deceased was buried in the 2nd Alley of Honors, and the mourning ceremony was held at the Tazapir Mosque.
