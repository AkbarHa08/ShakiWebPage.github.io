Rashid bey Efendiyev

Rashid bey Ismayil oglu Efendizadeh was born in 1863 in Sheki in a clergy family. He received his primary education from a teacher named Abdurrazaq Tahirzadeh in the mollakhana located in the Juma Mosque. After graduating from the mosque school in 1873, he continued his education in Russian, first at the emergency school, then at the city school. R. Efendiyev became interested in drama in the 80s of the 19th century while studying at the Gori Teachers' Seminary, wrote the first play "Blood Hearth" and staged it on the school stage. He is considered one of the first playwrights after Mirza Fatali Akhundov. Inspired by the success of this work, the author continued to work on art. During his ten years as a teacher in Gutgashen, for eight years as deputy secretary of the Tbilisi Department of Religious Affairs, and finally as a teacher at the Gori seminary for 16 years, he never strayed from writing, especially writing plays for education. Rashid Bey was one of the famous and hard-working teachers of Azerbaijan. He devoted his life to school, education, textbooks, and plays that appealed to children. His alphabet book "Kindergarten" and reading book "Basiratul-etfal" are among the first textbooks to become famous in Azerbaijan. He often went to Istanbul and published his textbooks with great difficulty. In addition to the, He wrote plays, arranged them on school stages in the first place, and even personally participated as an artist.
