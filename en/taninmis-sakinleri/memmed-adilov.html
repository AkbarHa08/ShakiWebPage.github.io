Mammad Adilov

Mammad Adilov was born on November 18, 1953 in the city of Nukha. He graduated from Baku State University (1976). In 1976-1977 he worked as a teacher at a secondary vocational school in Sheki, in 1977-1982 as a correspondent at the Azerbaijan Radio and "Baku" newspaper, in 1981-1989 as a teacher at two-year Arabic language courses at the Azerbaijan Institute of Foreign Languages. After serving as a translator officer in the army in 1982-1984, he became the director general of the Basic Library of the Azerbaijan Academy of Sciences in 1984 [1], the same year a senior laboratory assistant at the Institute of Manuscripts of the Azerbaijan Academy of Sciences, then a junior researcher, researcher, scientific secretary. served as deputy director. He also taught at Lankaran State University in 1993-1996, and at the Sumgayit branch of Baku Islamic University in 1995-1998. In 2003, he was elected director of the Institute of Manuscripts of the Azerbaijan National Academy of Sciences. In 1986 he defended his dissertation on "Abbreviations in the Azerbaijani language", in 2004 he defended his doctoral dissertation on "Azerbaijani paleography and historical orthography". He has published about 170 scientific works, two of them abroad, and participated in the preparation of more than 100 books for publication. He participated in ISESCO symposia in Rabat (1993) and Cairo (1996), as well as on scientific trips to Moscow, Istanbul, Tehran, Vilnius, Ashgabat, Mecca, Konya, Urmia, Kazan, Turkestan. In 1999, he was a member of the state commissions celebrating the 1,300th anniversary of the writing of "Kitabi-Dada Gorgud", and in 2003, the 800th anniversary of Khaja Nasreddin Tusi. He was a member of the expert council on philological sciences of the Supreme Attestation Commission under the President of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the editor-in-chief of the magazine "In the treasury of manuscripts". In 2003 he was awarded the Honorary Decree of ANAS, in 2005 he was awarded the "Progress" medal. Mammad Adilov arrived in Sheki on October 16, 2011 to attend a wedding party and attended the wedding, but died suddenly that night in Sheki after leaving the wedding.
