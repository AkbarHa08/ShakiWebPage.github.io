Mammad Efendiyev

Mammad Rashid oglu Efendiyev was born on October 5, 1887 in Sheki. In 1908 he entered the Faculty of Physics and Mathematics of St. Petersburg University and after graduating he worked as a teacher in Tbilisi, then as a teacher and director of the gymnasium in Sheki. After the establishment of Soviet power in Azerbaijan in 1920 was the organizer of the courses. In 1930, he worked as the head of the mathematics department at the Lenin API. M.Afendiyev was awarded the degree of Dean of the Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences (1934-1936) at API, Head of the Department of Mathematical Analysis at AUL (1935-1965), Professor. Personal belongings, photos, historical documents are protected in the funds of the National History Museum of Azerbaijan. In 1943 he was awarded the honorary title of Honored Scientist, in 1945 he was awarded the title of Professor. Efendiyev was the first to use the Latin script instead of the mathematical expressions common in the Middle East. For the first time, he gave lectures in Azerbaijani on the subjects of mathematical analysis, analytical geometry and methods of teaching mathematics. Efendiyev “Algebra program and task”, “Ordinary differential equations” (1950) and others. is the author of works. During the Soviet era, he was awarded the Order of Lenin and two Orders of the Red Banner of Labor. Efendiyev Mammad Rashid oglu died on June 1, 1977 in the city of Baku.
