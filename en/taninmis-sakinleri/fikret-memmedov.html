Fikrat Mammadov

Fikrat Mammadov graduated from the law faculty of the Moscow State University named after MV Lomonosov in 1977, and from the Baku APM (Academy of Public Administration) in 1988. He started his career in 1977 as an assistant to the district prosecutor, worked as a prosecutor of the investigation department in the Baku city prosecutor's office, senior prosecutor and head of the organizational control department in the republican prosecutor's office. Later, he was the head of the Legal Department of the Prosecutor General's Office, Sumgayit City Prosecutor, Deputy Prosecutor General in 1994-2000, and in April 2000 was appointed Minister of Justice of the Republic of Azerbaijan. Later in the formed governments F. Mammadov was re-appointed to this position 4 times. He has been the Chairman of the Judicial-Legal Council since 2005 and has been re-elected to this position several times. He is a state special adviser of justice of the first degree. It is noted that Mammadov has contributed to the improvement of the country's judicial system and its adaptation to international standards. He is a senator and honorary member of the International Association of Prosecutors, and vice president of the International Association of Anti-Corruption Bodies and the UN Congress on Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice.
