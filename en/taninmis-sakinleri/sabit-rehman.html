Sabit Rahman

Mahmudov Sabit (Sabit Rahman) (March 26, 1910, Nukha, Baku province, Russian Empire - September 23, 1970, Baku, Azerbaijan SSR) - Azerbaijani writer, playwright, honored art worker of Azerbaijan. Sabit Rahman was born on March 26, 1910 in Sheki (then Nukha). He began his literary career in 1926-1927 with the feuilletons published in the magazine of Molla Nasreddin. The first collection of stories - "Violent" was published in 1931. He is the author of many stories and plays. Sabit Rahman is one of the founders of Azerbaijani comedy. He wrote the world-famous "Arshin mal alan" and "O olmasin, bu olsun", "Koroglu", "Where is Ahmad?" He is the author of his films, screenplays of the Star Musical Comedy, libretto of the Seven Beautiful Ballets. In 1943, he was awarded the title of Honored Art Worker of the Azerbaijan SSR, twice awarded the Order of the Red Banner of Labor and medals. Sheki Drama Theater is named after S. Rahman. There are streets named after him in Baku and Sheki. Sabit Rahman died in 1970 in Baku.
