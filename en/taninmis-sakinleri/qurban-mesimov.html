Gurban Masimov

Gurban Masimov was born on January 26, 1971. In 1988-1993 he studied at the directing faculty of the State University of Culture and Arts named after A. Huseynzade, and in 1995-2001 at the vocal department of the Baku Music Academy. In 1998, he graduated from Robert Sturua's international art course in Tbilisi, Georgia. In 2000, he participated in the "Fildenkreis Method" seminar within the "Summer Academy" program in Sofia, Bulgaria. Since 1994, he has been working at the "Crazy Meeting" Pantomime theater-studio headed by Bakhtiyar Khanizade. In 2000, the theater received state status and was renamed the State Pantomime Theater. In 2000-2001, he was the host and director of the program "Nam-Nam" broadcast on ABA TV. In 2003, he continued the same program on another channel, Lider TV, under the name Show-Nam-Nam in the Kitchen. At present, he is the host of "Gulmashakar" program on Azerbaijan Television with wet typing. He also has creative experience in the clown genre. The stage nickname is Clown Appa. (2013) Member of the Union of Cinematographers of the Republic of Azerbaijan, since 2013 has been working as a chief director at the State Puppet Theater named after Abdulla Shaig. (2014) UNIMA - Member of the International Association of Puppet Theaters.
