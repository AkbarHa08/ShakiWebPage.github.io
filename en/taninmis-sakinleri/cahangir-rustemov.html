Jahangir Rustamov

Rustamov Jahangir Mahmud oglu was born on March 5, 1926 in the city of Sheki. He graduated from a 7-year school in 1939. In 1939–1941 he studied at the Azerbaijan State Art School named after A.Azimzade. After graduating from art school, Jahangir Rustamov entered the Moscow State Art Institute named after Surikov, but dropped out of school due to marital status and returned to Baku. The artist's work covers many genres of painting and is distinguished by its national spirit and poetics. Examples of this are the multi-figured plaques on the historical theme created by J.Rustamov, as well as compositions on the theme of holiday, labor and rest, as well as household paintings, epic landscapes, portraits and expressive still lifes. "Vineyard", "Tea growers", "National color, emotional" For his contribution to the development of fine arts in Azerbaijan, Jahangir Rustamov was awarded the title of Honored Artist of the Azerbaijan SSR in 1982, and People's Artist of the Republic of Azerbaijan in 2002. The artist's solo exhibition opened in 2002 at the V.Samadova Exhibition Hall in Baku. He was a personal pensioner of the President of the Republic of Azerbaijan in 2004. Jahangir Rustamov died in 2007.
