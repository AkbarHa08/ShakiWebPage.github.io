Ismail Osmanli

Ismayil Osmanli - Azerbaijani theater and film actor, People's Artist of the Azerbaijan SSR (1949), People's Artist of the USSR (1974). Ismayil Osmanli was born on April 11, 1902 in Nukha (Shaki) in a poor family. He did not have a perfect education. Orphaned at the age of eleven, Ismayil worked in a silk factory and tannery. In 1920, he became a member of the drama circle of the Workers 'and Peasants' Club in the district. He moved to Ganja in 1922 and worked at the amateur theater here for 6 years. In 1928, he was invited to the Tbilisi State Azerbaijan Drama Theater. He came to Baku in 1929 and was accepted to the troupe of the National Drama Theater and worked there until the end of his life. Ismayil Osmanli was a performer of characteristic characters in the genre of drama and tragedy, humorous characters in dramatic plays, as well as satirical and satirical roles in comedies. The main roles are: "Peasants" (Minister of Internal Affairs), "Two boys from one neighborhood" (Mahmud), "Let it be, let it be" (Rza bey), "True friend" (Master Shirali), "Invincible battalion" (Yusif) , "I was not beautiful" (Postmaster), "26s" (Shahbazov), "Stars do not end" (Haji Zeynalabdin Tagiyev), "Nasimi" (Naimi), "I want seven sons" (Kalantar) and other films .
