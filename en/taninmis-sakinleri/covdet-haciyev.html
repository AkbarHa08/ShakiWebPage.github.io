Covdat Hajiyev

Ahmad-Jovdat Ismayil oglu Hajiyev (June 5, 1917, Nukha, Yelizavetpol province - January 18, 2002, Baku) - Azerbaijani composer, rector and professor of the Azerbaijan State Conservatory, People's Artist of the Azerbaijan SSR (1960), Honored Art Worker of the Azerbaijan SSR. Jovdat Hajiyev was born in 1917 in the city of Nukha (Sheki). After graduating from high school, he studied at the Azerbaijan State Conservatory in 1935-1938, and at the Moscow Conservatory in 1938-1941, and later continued his education, which was incomplete during the war, and in 1947 graduated from the class of composer Dmitry Shostakovich. From 1947 to the end of his life, along with composing, he taught at the Azerbaijan State Conservatory, and in 1957-1969 he worked as the rector of this higher music school. Jovdat Hajiyev, who played a great role in the development of the Azerbaijani school of composition, also rendered unparalleled services in the training of professional musicians in Azerbaijan. He was a wonderful teacher who brought up several generations of Azerbaijani composers. Jovdat Hajiyev's services in the development and promotion of Azerbaijani music culture were highly appreciated. He was twice awarded the USSR State Prize, was awarded many orders and medals, including the highest award of the Republic of Azerbaijan - the Order of Glory. Jovdat Hajiyev died on January 18, 2002 at the age of 85. She is the wife of dancer Amina Dilbazi.
