Salman Mumtaz

Salman Mumtaz - Azerbaijani poet, literary critic, textologist and bibliographer, collector of medieval manuscripts, member of the Azerbaijan Writers' Union since 1934, researcher at the 1st category of the literature sector of the Institute of Language and Literature of the Azerbaijan Branch of the USSR Academy of Sciences, Azerbaijan State Scientific Research Institute Head of the Department of Azerbaijani Literature (1929-1932). Salman Mumtaz studied in Ashgabat. From 1910, he spoke against religious fanaticism and superstition with his poems and feuilletons in "Molla Nasreddin" and other magazines. In addition to his native Azerbaijani, he mastered Persian, Arabic, Russian, Turkish and Urdu. Salman Mumtaz Imadeddin Nasimi, Govsi Tabrizi, Molla Panah Vagif, Gasim bey Zakir, Mirza Shafi prepared the works of Vazeh and other Azerbaijani classics for publication. In 1927-1928, Azerbaijan published samples of ashug poetry in two volumes. He also wrote scientific-critical texts of Khatai, Fuzuli and others. Mumtaz, a victim of Stalin's repression, was arrested in October 1937 and sentenced to 10 years in prison. He was shot in Oryol in September 1941 while in prison. 270 manuscripts collected by him were destroyed during the arrest. Salman Mumtaza was acquitted by the Supreme Court of the USSR on November 17, 1956. Mumtaz, a victim of Stalin's repression, was arrested in October 1937 and sentenced to 10 years in prison.
