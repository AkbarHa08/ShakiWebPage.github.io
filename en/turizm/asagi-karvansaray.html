Ashagi Caravanserai

Ashagi Caravanserai - a caravanserai located in the territory of the ancient city of Sheki.

Location

It is located on Mirza Fatali Akhundov Street in Sheki. On the arched bank of the Gurjanchay there are a number of facilities - the Yuxari and Ashagi Caravanserais, which are the business center of the city. Caravanserais were an integral part of the feudal East, and all historians and travelers who came here always gave a special place to caravanserais when talking about Azerbaijan, and often described them.

History

As Sheki is one of the oldest cities of trade and crafts in Azerbaijan, many markets and caravanserais have been built here. Of the 5 large caravanserais in Sheki in the XVIII-XIX centuries ("Isfahan", "Tabriz", "Lezgi", "Armenian", "Fresh Caravanserai"), only two - the Yukhari Caravanserai and the Ashagi Caravanserai - have survived. Both monuments date back to the 18th century and were built by local craftsmen. These monuments were very comfortable and beautiful both in terms of construction and conditions, and had favorable conditions for large-scale transactions and purchases. It is also claimed that the Ashagi Caravanserai historical monument belongs to the XVI-XVIII centuries. Currently, about 35-40% of the building is lost.

The plan of the caravanserai

Sheki caravanserais, like others, were reserved for caravans and travelers and were built in accordance with their functional features. However, unlike caravanserais, in addition to rest rooms, special rooms have been built for conducting various trade operations and concluding exchange agreements. The caravanserais here characterize Shaki's architecture due to its plan structure, large size, and convenience for recreation and trade. The Lower Caravanserai, which is slightly larger than the Upper Caravanserai in terms of its total area, is 12 meters high on the street and 10 meters high on the east and west sides. Covering an area of ​​8,000 square meters, the Lower Caravanserai has 242 rooms and four entrance gates. This caravanserai also reflects the unique architectural style of the artists. The main facade of the caravanserai, which has three floors in the south-east and two floors in the yard, overlooks the river. This gives the building peace and prosperity. The facade of the caravanserai is characterized by horizontal sections, which divide the wall plane into three tiers arranged with arched windows, the arches of the windows differ slightly in shape. The pool is located in the center of the staff, which is surrounded by arched balcony overpasses. During the construction of the caravanserai, everything was taken into account for the convenience of merchants from distant lands, the safety of themselves and their goods. Although the gates are hospitably opened to travelers and merchants, when closed, they become an impregnable fortress. This once again confirms the high outlook, invaluable taste, incomparable architectural style, material and spiritual values ​​of Sheki artists.

About Shaki

Sheki is a city in the Republic of Azerbaijan, the administrative center of the Sheki region. It has been a republican city since January 4, 1963. Until 1846–15 February 1968, it was called Nukha. It is the administrative center of Sheki region. During the Soviet era, it had the status of a republican city.
