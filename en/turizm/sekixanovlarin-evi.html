Shakikhanovs' house

The house of the Shakikhanovs or the Shakikhanov Palace is one of the most unique monuments of Azerbaijani architecture, located in the reserve area of ​​Sheki.

Architecture

The Shakikhanovs' house is a form of transition from a public house to a palace-type house. The two-storey building, which has an elongated, rectangular shape, retains the features of Sheki people's houses, and the rich decorative elements in the interior make the building look like a palace-type building. The Shakikhanovs' house consists of two floors. Each floor of the house consists of three rooms and two small corridors, connected by rooms with stairs leading from the first floor to the second floor. As the rooms on the first floor are winter living rooms, "stoves" are placed here. The second floor, which is similar to the first floor, is designed for guests. The main place in the interior of the living room on the second floor is a horizontal decorative fireplace. The stove is decorated with beautiful pictures and the whole interior is symmetrically designed to match the interior of folk living. Shallow rectangular niches are placed on the sides of the stove. The niches are decorated with pictures of the heroes of the Azerbaijani poet Nizami's poems "Seven beauties", "Leyli and Majnun" and covered with a simple stalactite. There are two such niches in the walls of the skull, and between them are the doors to the adjoining rooms and the hall. A shelf passes over the niches and cavities of the three walls, which are also located on the stalactite cornice. The complex structure of the mirror stalactite is formed by the cornice passing through the upper part of the hall. On the shelves are small niches on the axis of the niches. These niches are decorated with bouquets of flowers, pictures of animals and birds.

About Shaki

Sheki is a city in the Republic of Azerbaijan, the administrative center of the Sheki region. It has been a republican city since January 4, 1963. Until 1846–15 February 1968, it was called Nukha. It is the administrative center of Sheki region. During the Soviet era, it had the status of a republican city.
